NEWS & NOTES

  1. 1
    KC: Mahomes on track for full training camp
    Patrick Mahomes is trending toward full 11-on-11 work to open camp, easing any Week 1 worry. The thing to watch is whether last year's career-high rushing — a big driver of his fantasy value — sticks around now that he's healthy.
  2. 2
    NO: Travis Etienne lands the full-volume role
    Etienne left Jacksonville for New Orleans and is expected to handle "full volume" out of the Saints' backfield, putting him back in every-down territory after splitting time last year.
  3. 3
    JAX: Bhayshul Tuten takes over the Jaguars backfield
    With Etienne gone, the second-year back sits atop the depth chart and has been flashing explosive plays in camp while Chris Rodriguez misses time. His 4.32 speed and short-yardage punch give him real upside in Liam Coen's offense.
  4. 4
    NE: A.J. Brown and Drake Maye click early
    The two have shown immediate chemistry this offseason, and Brown's presence should keep boxes lighter for the whole New England offense — including rookie back TreVeyon Henderson, whose improved pass protection is earning him more snaps.
  5. 5
    NYG: Cam Skattebo set to lead the Giants backfield
    Coming off his injury recovery, Skattebo is slated as New York's lead runner in 2026, with Tyrone Tracy and Devin Singletary working behind him. Head coach Jim Harbaugh has called him a top-tier back.
  6. 6
    HOU: David Montgomery inherits a clean goal-line role
    Montgomery moves to Houston as the early-down and short-yardage back with no real competition for touchdowns near the end zone. The one swing factor is a Texans offensive line that struggled to run-block last season.
  7. 7
    BAL: Lamar Jackson raves about Zay Flowers
    Jackson called Flowers' value "out of this world" this offseason. He's already an entrenched target hog in Baltimore — the one thing that could push him into the elite tier is a jump in touchdowns.
  8. 8
    DET: New coordinator points to a Sam LaPorta bounce-back
    Detroit brought in Drew Petzig — the play-caller who made Trey McBride a target monster in Arizona. That scheme change sets up a bigger role for LaPorta, who's been highly efficient whenever he's healthy.
  9. 9
    CLE: Harold Fannin Jr. keeps flashing in Cleveland
    The Browns lean on their tight ends, and Fannin backed up a strong rookie year (11.7 PPR points per game) even after the team added receivers. He tied rookie Christian McCaffrey for the most 5+ yard gains after contact by any player under 22 since 2015.
  10. 10
    BAL: Isaiah Likely lined up for a bigger role
    New play-caller Matt Nagy is expected to feature his move tight end more often. Likely has already topped 12.5 PPR points in eight of his nine career games with at least six targets — the volume just needs to follow.
